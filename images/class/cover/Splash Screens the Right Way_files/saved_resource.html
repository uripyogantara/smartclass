                        Scott Herter Zarah Khan • a year ago

                        There are two types of actions that can be taken, synchronous and asynchronous. A synchronous task occurs on the same thread and you wait until it is finished. An asynchronous task runs on a different thread and you continue after the call is made and run in parallel with the new task. It is possible for the main thread to finish before the other thread does. This means that if you issue an asynchronous call may still be running meaning that startup may not be finished before your splash screen goes away.

                        Depending on the task running in the background this could be no big deal or it could mean your UI is unresponsive after the splash screen goes away until the background task finished.

                                                      AStereo GeN-1 • a year ago

                                                      In the xml file drawable/background_splash, change the value --> android:gravity="center" to --> android:gravity="fill"

                                                      This will stretch the image out to the corners. Most screens use the same ratio, so just upscale the image after editing the resolution to your mobile and the image will adjust.
